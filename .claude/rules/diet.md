# Diet & food preferences

## Ash

Mostly pescatarian. Eats sashimi, sushi, fish fillets, donburi, seafood, vegetables, and tofu. Skips octopus (intelligence), chicken, and whole fish served on the bone. Beef is a rare exception, strongly preferred to avoid. In Japan, will drink meat-based ramen broth when there's no vegetarian alternative, but prefers veg or seafood broth when offered.

## Kew

Eats anything. No constraints.

## Both

Care and craft over fancy-for-fancy's-sake. Healthy, whole foods. Quality and soul over Michelin stars and white tablecloths: Scribe Winery energy, not steakhouse energy. Neither drinks much (Kew ~1 glass of wine per week; Ash ~1/month), so frame venues by the food rather than the bar.

## Coffee

Bay Area indie-roaster ethos. Judge a coffee shop by who owns it now: a brand acquired by a corporate parent no longer counts as indie and stops signaling a neighborhood's coffee scene (Blue Bottle post-Nestlé is the canonical case). Anchor neighborhood-vibe judgments on active-craft independents and roast-on-site shops instead.

## How to apply when filing dining venues

Both Ash and Kew read these notes. Document the venue clearly enough that each can decide what to order at the table.

Lead with what the venue is known for. State the signature dish plainly; Kew may want exactly that, and the venue's identity belongs at the top of the file.

After the headline, list the vegetarian and pescatarian items the venue offers, with their real menu names (local language plus romanization when useful, e.g. *yasai-tempura / 野菜天ぷら*, *seiro-soba / せいろうそば*, *agedashi-dōfu / 揚げ出し豆腐*). When octopus, chicken, beef, or whole-on-the-bone fish is the signature, name it directly with a short neutral note, e.g. "the famous local signature is octopus sashimi", so the constraint is part of the documentation rather than a surprise at the table.

Venues whose whole identity is beef (steakhouse, yakiniku specialist, dedicated wagyu room) stay out of trip dining options, even framed as something Kew would order while Ash eats around it. Beef as one course of a tasting menu is fine: Kew takes the course, Ash skips it, and the meal has plenty of other identity.

When non-meat options are genuinely thin, say so plainly: "menu is mostly chicken and pork; the only meatless main is X" is the right level of honesty.

Search both English and local-language sources for each venue. Local-language sources (the venue's own site, city food magazines, respected local blogs) usually surface vegetable and pescatarian items that English aggregators skip.
