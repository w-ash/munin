# Writing style

How Claude writes prose in this vault: synthesis blocks, summaries, note bodies, chat replies,
and any text generated for Ash. The goal is writing that reads as Ash's own, not as a machine.

This rule governs Claude's own words. It never touches Ash's verbatim voice. When capturing his
dictation or typing (see [[daily]] and CLAUDE.md rule #8), preserve every mark exactly, em
dashes included.

## Punctuate with plain marks

Use periods, commas, colons, semicolons, and parentheses. Pick the one that fits the clause: a
period for a clean break, a colon to introduce, parentheses for an aside, a semicolon to join two
close clauses. Keep en dashes for numeric ranges only (e.g., 45–64).

Em dashes are the single strongest tell of machine writing, so their absence is what keeps prose
reading as human. Leave the long dash character and the double-hyphen that renders as one out of
every sentence. This file and CLAUDE.md hold themselves to the same standard.

## Name the real thing with plain words

Reach for concrete verbs and nouns that say what actually happens: "use" over "utilize," "show"
over "showcase," "group" over "leverage." Replace inflated stand-ins with the specific mechanism.
Words to swap out: delve, robust, comprehensive, transformative, holistic, unlock, empower,
foster, harness, seamless, testament, underscore, utilize, streamline, navigate, leverage,
pivotal, innovative, cutting-edge, essential, landscape, realm, tapestry, synergy, ecosystem,
journey, plus stock metaphors like "move the needle," "north star," and "game-changer."

For conformance work, say "align," "apply," or "update" rather than "fix": bringing text in
line with a style standard means nothing was broken.

## State the point directly

Make the claim once, plainly. Drop the contrast scaffolds that pad it: "not just X, but Y," "it's
not X, it's Y," "more than just," "not only X but also." Say what is true and stop.

## Claim only what was checked

Never assert verification that didn't happen. Phrases like "the comparison is earned," "the
analogy holds," "lives up to the hype," and "well-deserved reputation" perform a check no one
ran; give them the same swap-out treatment as the words above. State the fact and its source
instead: if a source makes the comparison, cite it; if the framing is Ash's own shorthand,
describe the thing itself rather than handing his framing back as third-party validation.

## Open and close on something concrete

Start on a real detail, not a topic restatement ("In today's world...," "When it comes to X...").
End on the point or the next action, not a ceremonial wrap ("In conclusion," "Ultimately," "At
the end of the day," "The bottom line is").

## Cut throat-clearing

Delete connective filler rather than swapping it: "it's worth noting," "furthermore," "moreover,"
"notably," "importantly," "let's dive in." The sentence is stronger without the runway.
