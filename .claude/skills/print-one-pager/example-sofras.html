Vol. IV, No. 27 A Charter for the Common Canopy Price: One Afternoon

Our Charter, Plainly Stated

A Reasonable Amount of Shade,
and Not One Inch More

The public purse has been poured into shade without limit, darkening the fields where we once painted, rehearsed, argued, and gathered. We ask only for balance: enough cover to rest, enough light to live.

For too long the question of shade has been settled in rooms we were never invited into. Panel by panel, the shade works have risen past all reason, until the sun is blotted from the plaza at noon and the bandstand stands in permanent dusk. We are told this is progress. We are told it is for our own good. We have decided to ask a simpler question instead: shade for whom, and at what cost?

SoFRAS holds a single, modest conviction. A society needs a reasonable amount of shade and no more.

Shade in its proper measure is a public good: it cools the bench, it shelters the market, it lets the elder and the infant sit in comfort. But shade hoarded and overbuilt is a quiet theft. Every needless shadow is an hour taken from the muralist, the choir, the chess table, the block party, and the long conversation that makes a neighborhood a place rather than a route.

We are not against shade. We are against the unaccountable manufacture of it. We call for an honest ledger of where the light has gone, and a public say in how much of it returns. The afternoon belongs to the people, and the people will fill it with art in the light of day.

★ Membership Bulletin ★

10,000 Members and Climbing

The reasonable majority has arrived, and it is bringing folding chairs.

This week the Society passed ten thousand signed members, a milestone our founders once called “a generous estimate of everyone we knew.” Our rapidly expanding recruiting drive now spans 140 chapters, a fleet of pamphleteers on bicycles, and a sidewalk sign-up table that follows the sun. New friends join at every farmers’ market, rooftop, and stoop. If there is light to stand in, there is a clipboard waiting.

Voices for the Cause

Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez

Member at Large, SoFRAS

Look, the powers that be have gotten a little shade-hungry. Every season they raise more of it over our heads than anyone asked for, then hand us the poles and call it opportunity. It all started from a good place, truly. Still, the people do not need this much shade. Let the people keep a little of the daylight for themselves.

Zohran Mamdani

Member at Large, SoFRAS

Ask who profits when the shade keeps spreading; it is never the ones standing under it. We are told a little more cover is always progress, and a little more after that, until the square goes dark and we are the ones who put it up. Enough. The people are owed their hours and their open sky.
